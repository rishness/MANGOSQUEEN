    About Mangosteen

    Definition & History

    "Garciana Mangostana" or known as Mangosteen is a sweet tropical fruit native to Southeast Asia. Known as the "Queen of Fruits," that has been cultivated for centuries in the Malay Archipelago and the Philippines.

    Nutritional Value

    Rich in antioxidants called xanthones and provides anti-inflammatory benefits, Vitamin C, and fiber. It boosts immunity and improves skin health while being low in calories, also supports heart health by promoting better blood circulation.

    Commonly Found

    Globally found in Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, and India. In the Philippines, it is abundant in Mindanao (Davao, Sulu, Zamboanga, Cotabato, Agusan) and cultivated in Luzon areas like Mindoro, Laguna, Quezon, and Cavite.

    ANTHRACNOSE

    Fungal infection causing dark lesions and fruit rot.

    DRY

    Dehydrated fruit with wrinkled skin and hard texture.

    GUMMOSIS

    Secretion of gummy substances from the fruit surface.

    HALF RIPE

    Fruit with uneven ripening characteristics.

    INSECT DAMAGE

    Holes or markings caused by various pests.

    RAW

    Immature fruit harvested too early.

    RIPE

    Perfect condition fruit with smooth, unblemished skin.

    ROT

    Decaying fruit tissue with discolored areas.

    OVER RIPE

    Excessively mature fruit with reduced shelf life.
