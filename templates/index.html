    About Mangosteen

    What is Mangosteen?

    Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) is a tropical evergreen tree valued for its sweet, tangy, and juicy fruit. Often called the "queen of fruits," it has a thick purple rind and soft white segments inside.

    Origin & History

    Native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Malay Archipelago. Historically cultivated in Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and Philippines for centuries. Introduced to other tropical regions in the 19th century.

    Nutritional Value

    Rich in antioxidants, particularly xanthones. Contains vitamin C, fiber, and various minerals. Low in calories but high in nutrients, making it a valuable addition to a healthy diet.

    Economic Importance

    High-value export crop for tropical countries. Fresh mangosteen commands premium prices in international markets. Processed products include juices, canned fruit, and supplements.

    Common Diseases

    Susceptible to various diseases including fruit rot, leaf blight, and stem canker. Major concerns include Phytophthora root rot and anthracnose which can significantly impact yield.

    Growing Regions

    Primarily grown in Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, and Vietnam. Also cultivated in parts of South America, Africa, and Australia where tropical conditions permit.

    Mangosteen Classifications

    Know Your Mangosteen Quality

    Learn about the different quality classifications of mangosteen – plus how to identify and sort them effectively.

    ANTHRACNOSE

    Fungal infection causing dark lesions and fruit rot.

    DRY

    Dehydrated fruit with wrinkled skin and hard texture.

    RIPE

    Perfect condition fruit with smooth, unblemished skin.

    GUMMOSIS

    Secretion of gummy substances from the fruit surface.

    OVER RIPE

    Excessively mature fruit with reduced shelf life.

    HALF RIPE

    Fruit with uneven ripening characteristics.

    RAW

    Immature fruit harvested too early.

    ROT

    Decaying fruit tissue with discolored areas.

    INSECT DAMAGE

    Holes or markings caused by various pests.
