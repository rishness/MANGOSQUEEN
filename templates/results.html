Detailed Information

{% if disease_name == 'Anthracnose' %} ANTHRACNOSE

Anthracnose is a fungal disease that causes dark lesions on fruit surfaces and can lead to rot.

Prevention and Treatment

  • Remove and destroy all infected plant material to prevent spread
  • Apply copper-based fungicides every 7-14 days during wet seasons
  • Improve air circulation by proper pruning and spacing of trees
  • Avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness periods
  • Harvest fruits carefully to minimize injuries that allow fungal entry
{% elif disease_name == 'Dry Rot' %} DRY ROT

Dry rot affects post-harvest fruits, leading to dehydration, hardening, and decay.

Prevention and Management

  • Store fruits in cool (10-13°C), dry conditions with good ventilation
  • Sort and remove damaged fruits before storage
  • Use clean, sanitized containers to prevent contamination
  • Apply postharvest fungicide dips if available and approved
  • Monitor stored fruits regularly and remove any showing early signs
{% elif disease_name == 'Gummosis' %} GUMMOSIS

Gummosis is a condition where the tree exudes gum due to stress, infections, or physical injury.

Treatment and Prevention

  • Carefully scrape away diseased bark until healthy tissue is reached
  • Disinfect wounds with copper fungicide or approved tree paint
  • Improve drainage if soil is waterlogged around the tree
  • Avoid mechanical injuries to trunk and major branches
  • Maintain proper nutrition and watering to reduce tree stress
{% elif disease_name == 'Half ripe' %} HALF RIPE FRUIT

This mangosteen is in a mid-ripening stage. Allow it to ripen fully before consumption or sale.

Handling Recommendations

  • Store at room temperature (20-25°C) away from direct sunlight
  • Place with ethylene-producing fruits like bananas to accelerate ripening
  • Check daily by gently pressing - ripe when slightly soft
  • Once ripe, refrigerate to slow further ripening (10-13°C)
  • Handle carefully to avoid bruising which leads to uneven ripening
{% elif disease_name == 'Healthy ripe' %} HEALTHY RIPE FRUIT

This mangosteen is ripe and in excellent condition. Enjoy fresh or store appropriately.

Storage and Handling

  • Store in refrigerator at 10-13°C with high humidity if possible
  • Consume within 3-5 days for best quality and flavor
  • Place in single layer or use dividers to prevent bruising
  • Wash only immediately before consumption to prevent mold
  • For longer storage, consider freezing pulp in airtight containers
{% elif disease_name == 'Over ripe' %} OVER RIPE FRUIT

This fruit has passed its optimal ripeness stage. Use promptly or repurpose for processing.

Usage Recommendations

  • Inspect carefully - discard if showing mold or fermentation
  • Use immediately in smoothies, juices, or purees
  • Consider making jams, sauces, or desserts that require cooking
  • Remove any discolored or fermented sections before use
  • Refrigerate if not using immediately (consume within 24 hours)
{% elif disease_name == 'Raw' %} RAW FRUIT

This mangosteen is not yet ripe. Let it mature naturally before consumption.

Ripening Process

  • Leave at room temperature (20-25°C) out of direct sunlight
  • Place in paper bag with ethylene-producing fruits to speed ripening
  • Check daily - ripe when color deepens and fruit yields slightly to pressure
  • Do not refrigerate until fully ripe as cold stops ripening process
  • Expect ripening to take 2-5 days depending on initial maturity
