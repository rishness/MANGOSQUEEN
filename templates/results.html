Additional Information

{% if disease_name == 'Anthracnose' %}

ANTHRACNOSE

Anthracnose is a fungal disease that causes dark lesions on fruit surfaces and can lead to rot.

Prevention and Treatment:

  1. Remove and destroy all infected plant material to prevent spread
  2. Apply copper-based fungicides every 7-14 days during wet seasons
  3. Improve air circulation by proper pruning and spacing of trees
  4. Avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness periods
  5. Harvest fruits carefully to minimize injuries that allow fungal entry
{% elif disease_name == 'Dry Rot' %}

DRY ROT

Dry rot affects post-harvest fruits, leading to dehydration, hardening, and decay.

Prevention and Management:

  1. Store fruits in cool (10-13°C), dry conditions with good ventilation
  2. Sort and remove damaged fruits before storage
  3. Use clean, sanitized containers to prevent contamination
  4. Apply postharvest fungicide dips if available and approved
  5. Monitor stored fruits regularly and remove any showing early signs
{% elif disease_name == 'Gummosis' %}

GUMMOSIS

Gummosis is a condition where the tree exudes gum due to stress, infections, or physical injury.

Treatment and Prevention:

  1. Carefully scrape away diseased bark until healthy tissue is reached
  2. Disinfect wounds with copper fungicide or approved tree paint
  3. Improve drainage if soil is waterlogged around the tree
  4. Avoid mechanical injuries to trunk and major branches
  5. Maintain proper nutrition and watering to reduce tree stress
{% elif disease_name == 'Half ripe' %}

HALF RIPE FRUIT

This mangosteen is in a mid-ripening stage. Allow it to ripen fully before consumption or sale.

Handling Recommendations:

  1. Store at room temperature (20-25°C) away from direct sunlight
  2. Place with ethylene-producing fruits like bananas to accelerate ripening
  3. Check daily by gently pressing - ripe when slightly soft
  4. Once ripe, refrigerate to slow further ripening (10-13°C)
  5. Handle carefully to avoid bruising which leads to uneven ripening
{% elif disease_name == 'Healthy ripe' %}

HEALTHY RIPE FRUIT

This mangosteen is ripe and in excellent condition. Enjoy fresh or store appropriately.

Storage and Handling:

  1. Store in refrigerator at 10-13°C with high humidity if possible
  2. Consume within 3-5 days for best quality and flavor
  3. Place in single layer or use dividers to prevent bruising
  4. Wash only immediately before consumption to prevent mold
  5. For longer storage, consider freezing pulp in airtight containers
{% elif disease_name == 'Over ripe' %}

OVER RIPE FRUIT

This fruit has passed its optimal ripeness stage. Use promptly or repurpose for processing.

Usage Recommendations:

  1. Inspect carefully - discard if showing mold or fermentation
  2. Use immediately in smoothies, juices, or purees
  3. Consider making jams, sauces, or desserts that require cooking
  4. Remove any discolored or fermented sections before use
  5. Refrigerate if not using immediately (consume within 24 hours)
{% elif disease_name == 'Raw' %}

RAW FRUIT

This mangosteen is not yet ripe. Let it mature naturally before consumption.

Ripening Process:

  1. Leave at room temperature (20-25°C) out of direct sunlight
  2. Place in paper bag with ethylene-producing fruits to speed ripening
  3. Check daily - ripe when color deepens and fruit yields slightly to pressure
  4. Do not refrigerate until fully ripe as cold stops ripening process
  5. Expect ripening to take 2-5 days depending on initial maturity
